-- What You Have To Know --

Gears You Need for Billairds


Cue Stick

  • Tip, Shaft, Butt, Grip/Wrap, Bumper

- A cue stick is made up of different parts that are branded. Cues are usually provided by your local pool places.

Billiard Balls

  • Solids, Stripes, Cue ball
  • Sets

- We use these different balls for a standard game of pool. The way which these balls are racked may differ based on the game being played.

The Table

  • Pockets, Felt, Sizes

- Table sizes may vary depends on the space available or the type of game being played; one with no holes, which is specifically used for carom. Ball sizes also change depends on the table and pocket sizes.

The Rack

  • Triangle Rack
  • Diamond Rack
  • Magic Rack

These racks have their own use:
- Triangle rack is mostly used in a game of 8-ball and 10-ball
- Diamond rack is made to be used for a game of 9-ball
- Magic Rack is made out of a thin plastic. It can be used for all three games mentioned above.

Extras

  • Chalk
  • Gloves
  • Case
  • Extra Tip

- Chalks are a crucial part of the game. They help a lot to avoid miscues.
- Gloves help your cue to glide smoothly, especially if your hands get sweaty.
- Cases are important if you have your own cue. This is to keep your stick secure and undamaged.
- Bringing an extra tip with you would be helpful as well. Cue tips are constantly at wear during game.
